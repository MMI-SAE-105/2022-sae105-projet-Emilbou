Fukushima

Fukushima, natural disaster and nuclear accident

Recognized as the most serious nuclear disaster since that of Chernobyl in 1986, the accident is one of the greatest disasters of the contemporary era.

Indeed, the Fukushima nuclear disaster is without doubt one of the most terrible events of the last 20 years.

It was on March 11, 2011, in the city of Fukushima in the Japanese archipelago that the accident occurred. Everything starts with an earthquake of magnitude 9 which in turn generates a tsunami with waves up to 14 meters high. With waves powerful enough to reach the nuclear power plant, the event seemed inevitable. The power plant lost its generators to ensure the cooling of the reactors, which caused a snowball effect leading to a massive release of radioactive effluents and the explosion of the plant.

Due to natural disasters taking place at the same time, the figures on injuries related to the explosion are constantly changing, even more than 11 years after the fact.

On the other hand, according to an article from 2018 of liberation, there is talk of a heavy death toll of 19,630 caused largely by the natural disasters themselves, a balance sheet therefore global of this tragic event.

The company in charge of operating the plant at the time was the energy company TEPCO, and some of its senior executives claim to have neglected civil security by underestimating the tsunami. Four senior executives of the company were subsequently fined 95 billion euros in damages for their responsibility in the nuclear disaster.

The event is classified 7/7 on the international scale of nuclear events, since April 12, 2011. This makes that today, Fukushima is recognized in the world as the city that suffered these disasters...
