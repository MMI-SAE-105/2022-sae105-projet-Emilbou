WA, the essence of Japanese design

Wa, the essence of Japanese design, a design book with historical significance

"Contemporary Japanese design is now at a turning point in its history and the time for major changes has come. They define the new directions for the coming decades and herald a new era, both technical and spiritual."

Tracing the history of Japanese design and its evolution, WA, the essence of Japanese design is a book immersing us as well in the classics of the Japanese artistic essence as in its contemporaries through some 250 pictures all respecting a strict and minimalist artistic nomenclature making the book this regular aspect.

Through everyday objects as well as less common objects such as paper shoes "kami no kuni" and tunics reflecting at best the Japanese culture or even a photograph of a soy sauce bottle. Wa, brings with textual coherence a significant amount of information that, taken separately, would not have made any logical sense at first.

This irregularity of objects does not prevent a very worked aesthetic aspect of the latter. With its set of colors rather closed and redundant, Rossella Menegazzo (professor of history and philosophy) and Stefania Piotti (historian of Japanese art) allow us to immerse ourselves in their book thanks to these famous shades of black and red. Very aware of the history of Japanese art, the book highlights both the successes and failures with the main goal of an evolution of current art. With a real philosophical dimension, we are informed from the first sentences that this book is only a springboard for future artistic innovations, relying on the notable advances of recent years.
