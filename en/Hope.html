Sino-Japanese relations

Sino-Japanese relations, historical conflicts, and possible reconciliation

Recognized as the most serious nuclear disaster since that of Chernobyl in 1986, the accident is one of the greatest disasters of the contemporary era.

Focusing on a truly geopolitical scope, I will try to draw up a synthetic portrait of Sino-Japanese relations as well as the hopes that are linked to it. The Sino-Japanese wars are often taken as an example of historical conflict because they can be traced back to before the 13th century. The Sino-Japanese wars are often taken as an example of historical conflict because they can be traced back to before the 13th century, from the multiple attempts of Mongolian China to invade the Japanese archipelago in the 13th century to more recent events such as the Sino-Japanese war of 1894 to 1895 for the control of Korea, or the war of 1937-1945 provoked by the invasion of the eastern part of China by the Japanese imperial army.

Since then, these same diplomatic relations have only deteriorated due to the Japan/US friendship. Today, with the conflicting historical relations between these two states, the latter are looking at each other in awe. Moreover, in recent years we have seen a massive rearmament of the Chinese arsenal which, according to some specialists, could worry Japan. For the future, my "hope" would be a total reconciliation of these two states in order to avoid new conflicts which would then put them in danger.
