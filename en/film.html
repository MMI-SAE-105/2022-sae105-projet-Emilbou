Howl's Moving Castle

Howl's Moving Castle, , a legendary and timeless animated film.

Part of the prestigious lineage of Studio Ghibli's animated films, The Moving Castle is a cinematic work that perfectly adapts the novel by Diana Wynne Jones, The Castle of Howl. Released on December 20, 2004 and lasting approximately two hours, The Moving Castle is an animated film produced by Toshio Suzuki.

We are plunged into a fictional world, with medieval allure and fantastic aspects. We quickly find a young woman of 18, Sophie, who is determined to spend the rest of her life in misery since the disappearance of her beloved father. She will meet by chance, a young wizard, Hauru in the middle of a chase, and will befriend him very quickly. When she learns of this friendship, a witch who is infatuated with Hauru, bewitches Sophie by transforming her into an old woman, making her lose her beauty. Unable to reveal her identity to Hauru, the two companions will have to overcome many incredible adventures.

The director of the adaptation, Hayao Miyazaki, born January 5, 1941 in Tokyo, is a Japanese animator, director, producer, screenwriter, writer and mangaka.
